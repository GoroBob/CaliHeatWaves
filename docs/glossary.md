# Glossary

**Aguablanca**: Colloquial name for a group of low-income comunas (13, 14, 15, 21) in southwestern Cali characterized by high population density, informal settlement patterns, and elevated surface temperatures.

**Apparent Temperature**: A heat index that combines air temperature, relative humidity, and wind speed to represent the perceived thermal stress on the human body.

**Comuna**: An administrative division within Cali (22 total), used as the unit of analysis for spatial epidemiology.

**DANE EEVV**: Estadísticas Vitales (Vital Statistics) microdata collected by Colombia's National Statistics Department, including births, deaths, and cause of death.

**DLNM**: Distributed Lag Non-Linear Model; a statistical method that estimates non-linear dose-response relationships and captures lagged effects over time, commonly used in environmental epidemiology.

**Heat Index**: A measure of perceived temperature combining air temperature and humidity; indicates heat stress risk to human health.

**Heatwave**: A prolonged period of abnormally high temperatures defined locally based on the climate and population adaptation capacity of a region.

**Tropical Night**: A night with a minimum temperature above a locally defined threshold (often 20°C), indicating insufficient nighttime cooling for human thermoregulation.

**UTCI**: Universal Thermal Climate Index; a bioclimatic index that accounts for temperature, humidity, wind speed, and radiation to represent thermal stress experienced by a reference person in a standardized outdoor setting.

**Urban Heat Island**: The phenomenon where urban areas experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural areas due to land-use change, reduced vegetation, and heat-absorbing built surfaces.
